# 100 Researched Greenfield Ideas

These are backlog-quality wedges for Greenfield, not fully written opportunity records yet.

I did a light current scan first so these are anchored to real shifts rather than pure brainstorming. The screen was not "zero competition"; it was "no obvious category owner for this exact wedge in a quick current sweep." Where a broad category looked crowded, I narrowed the idea to the last-mile workflow, evidence layer, or underserved buyer.

## Research Notes

- `CMS`: prior-authorization response, metrics, and denial-reason requirements begin on **January 1, 2026** for parts of the CMS Interoperability and Prior Authorization Final Rule, with additional drug-related proposals published in April 2026.
- `European Commission`: the European Accessibility Act entered into application on **June 28, 2025**, which creates compliance pressure for private-sector digital products selling into Europe.
- `EPA`: HFC technology-transition restrictions started hitting equipment categories on **January 1, 2025**; the 2024 Lead and Copper Rule Improvements also push water systems toward full lead service-line replacement over the next decade.
- `NERC` and `FERC`: AI/data-center load growth and large-load interconnection pressure are now explicit grid-planning topics, not niche edge cases.
- `USDA APHIS`: the National Milk Testing Strategy and related H5N1 testing requirements remain active in 2026.
- `DOL`: updated Registered Apprenticeship guidance was published on **March 9, 2026**, with a stated goal of faster sponsor determinations.
- `FDA`: the Food Traceability Rule is still strategically important, but enforcement was pushed out to **July 20, 2028**, which means there is time for prep tools rather than panic tooling.
- `FAA`: Remote ID is now a baked-in operational requirement; Part 107 waiver documentation remains a real burden for commercial operators.
